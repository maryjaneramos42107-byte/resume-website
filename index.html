How to Build a Résumé That Gets Noticed

Step-by-step guidance for clean layout, strong language, and high-impact structure.

See Example Résumé

What to Include

  • Header: Name, title (optional), city/state.
  • Summary: A concise 1–2 sentence professional intro.
  • Education: Degree, school, coursework.
  • Experience: Achievements with measurable results.
  • Skills: Tools, software, languages.
  • Awards/Projects: Strong supporting highlights.

How to Write It

  1. Choose a clean, readable one-page layout.
    • Use 10–12 pt body text.
    • Limit long paragraphs; prefer bullet points.
  2. Use action verbs + measurable results.
  3. Tailor one line to the role you want.
  4. Proofread thoroughly.

How to Build Experiences Worth Listing

Volunteer & Service

Community projects build leadership and initiative—valuable résumé boosters.

Side Projects

Personal projects give concrete examples to link in applications.

Classwork & Research

Group assignments and presentations demonstrate collaboration and skill.
